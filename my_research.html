FIRE-2 Simulations + Epoch of Reionization

Dwarf Galaxies in the FIRE-2 Simulations

The morphological evolution of dwarf galaxies is a fascinating mystery. Do these dwarf galaxies begin as rotationally supported, disky galaxies, like their spiral counterparts? Do they instead start as puffy, dispersion dominated systems? My work with Dr. Andrew Graus used Milky Way-satellite mass dwarf galaxies from the FIRE-2 simluations to augment past work (Wheeler et. al 2018). Our main findings can be seen above. We found that none of the simulated galaxies are rotationally supported. This suggests that the transition to rotational support happens at a mass greater than 108.6 solar masses. I presented this research at the UT Undergradute Research Forum.

MgII Escape in a local Lyc Leaker

The Epoch of Reionization represents a key part of time in the evolution of the Universe. The sources of reionizing photons and how these photons escaped their environments dictates how strucures formed and evolved. While working with Dr. John Chisholm, I measured how MgII escape fractions vary spatially in a local (z~0.4) galaxy. From our results in the plot above, we find that MgII escape fractions do vary spatially. This scatter between apertures implies that an observation of any one galaxy is simply an average of all its Lyc escape. This suggests that a large amount of observations are required to constrain the inherent scatter introduced by the random viewing angles of observed galaxies. These results should be published soon.
